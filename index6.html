AVANT-GARDE

FAUVISM

Early 20th. Century Characteristics: • Bold, Vibrant colors. • Simplified forms • Flatness and Two-Dimensionality • Subjective Expression • Independent color • Freedom • Artistic freedom and experimentation.

CUBISM

Early 20th century Transformed traditional notions of representation by breaking down objects and figures into geometric shapes and presenting multiple angles and viewpoints. • Geometric shapes • Interlocking planes • Mixed media • Reconsider perceptions of space and forms. • Abstraction • Broken mirror effect

FUTURISM

Early 20th. Century / Italy It celebrated modernity, technology and the dynamism of urban life. Characteristics: • Technology • Dynamic and fragmented forms • Geometric shapes and angular lines. • Emphasis on speed and motion • Controversial and provocative

EXPRESSIONISM

Early 20th. Century / Germany Art movement that sought to capture the subjective experience of the Morden world through bold colors, distorted forms and intense emotional expression. Characteristics: • Emotional intensity • Bold and vivid colors • Distorted forms • Subjectivity and symbolism • Dinamic brushwork • Focus on the human condition • Artistic freedom and experimentation.

SURREALISM

Surrealism is an artistic and literary movement that emerged in the early 20th century, known for its exploration of the unconscious mind, dreams, and irrational elements. Characteristics: • Artistic movement exploring unconscious • dreams • irrational elements • inspired by Freud.

FLUXUS

Movement of the 1960s characterized by experimental. Characteristics: • interdisciplinary • experimental • anti-art • conceptual • playful • performance-oriented • collaborative • embraces everyday objects • subversive.

DADAISM

Emerged during World War I, in Switzerland. Reaction against rationalism, nationalism and violence of the time. Characteristics: • Anti-art • Absurdity and irrationality • Cultural and political critique • Chance and spontaneity • Mockery and parody • Internationalism and collaboration

CONSTRUCTIVE

Learning theory where learners actively build understanding through experiences and interactions. Characteristics: • Active engagement • Prior knowledge • Social interaction • Meaning making • Authentic tasks • Reflection • Problem-solving • Inquiry-based • Flexible learning paths • Metacognition

Avant-garde art is a term that refers to innovative or experimental movements in art, typically associated with pushing the boundaries of artistic expression and challenging established norms.

    CHARACTERISTICS:

  • Innovation
  • Experimentation
  • Rejection of tradition
  • Provocation
  • Social of political commentary
  • TransgressionCollaboration
  • Anti-Commercialism
